Notes

Welcome to my notes — a collection of thoughts, ideas, and explorations at the intersection of physics, mathematics, and AI.

I hope these notes offer something useful or interesting. Some posts are polished; others are rough and half-formed. That’s intentional — good ideas rarely arrive fully formed.

I’m particularly inspired by Xiaogang Wen’s perspective on developing fundamental, field-changing ideas:

“To make revolutionary advances in physics, we cannot allow our imaginations to be trapped by the formalism. We cannot allow the formalism to define the boundary of our imagination.”

I’ve been fortunate to take his condensed matter physics course, where he emphasizes the importance of painting bold pictures — ideas that may sound wild at first — rather than making incremental refinements within existing formal frameworks.

I’m also deeply influenced by Alexander Grothendieck, who reshaped algebraic geometry by introducing entirely new ways of thinking. His philosophy resonates strongly with me: instead of merely decorating existing “houses” of thought, we should aim to build new ones.

This page reflects that spirit — a place to explore ideas freely, even when they are incomplete.
